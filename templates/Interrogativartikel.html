German Was für ein(e) and Welche(r): The Easy Guide

"Was für ein(e)" and "welche(r)" are fun ways to ask about stuff in German—like what something is like or which one it is. They’re perfect for questions (like "What kind of dog?") and sometimes sneak into regular sentences too.

1. Was für ein(e) – "What kind of…?"

What It’s For

It’s like asking, "What’s this thing like?" or "What type is it?"—you want to know more, like if it’s big, small, fun, or boring. Example: Was für ein Hund? means "What kind of dog?" (Fluffy? Mean? Tiny?)

How It Works

  • Say was für plus ein (for boys’ things), eine (for girls’ things), or nothing (for lots of things), then the word.
  • Boy stuff (like "dog" = Hund): Was für ein Hund?
  • Girl stuff (like "bag" = Tasche): Was für eine Tasche?
  • Lots of stuff (like "people" = Leute): Was für Leute?
  • In questions: Was für ein(e) + thing + action—like Was für ein Buch liest du? (What kind of book are you reading?)
  • In normal talk (not super common): It’s like "Wow, what a…!"—Das ist was für ein Tag! (What a day!)

When You Use It

Asking about stuff: Great for finding out what something’s like. Talking big: Adds a “whoa!” feeling in regular sentences.

Examples to Get It

Questions

  1. Was für ein Hund läuft da? (What kind of dog is running there?—big? small?)
  2. Was für eine Tasche hast du? (What kind of bag do you have?—fancy? old?)
  3. Was für Leute kommen? (What kind of people are coming?—nice? loud?)

Normal Sentences

  1. Das ist was für ein Hund! (That’s what kind of dog!—cool or crazy!)
  2. Er hat was für ein Auto! (He’s got what kind of car!—amazing!)

2. Welche(r) – "Which…?"

What It’s For

It’s like asking, "Which one?"—you’re picking something specific from a bunch, like choosing a favorite. Example: Welcher Hund? means "Which dog?" (This one? That one?)

How It Works

  • Say welcher (for boys’ things), welche (for girls’ things or lots of things), or welches (for kid stuff), then the word.
  • Boy stuff (like "dog" = Hund): Welcher Hund?
  • Girl stuff (like "bag" = Tasche): Welche Tasche?
  • Kid stuff (like "child" = Kind): Welches Kind?
  • Lots of stuff (like "people" = Leute): Welche Leute?
  • In questions: Welche(r) + thing + action—like Welches Buch liest du? (Which book are you reading?)
  • In normal talk (not super usual): It’s like wondering out loud—Er liest welches Buch? (He’s reading which book?—huh?)

When You Use It

Asking to pick: Perfect when you want one exact answer. Wondering: Adds a “which one?” vibe in regular talk.

Examples to Get It

Questions

  1. Welcher Hund läuft da? (Which dog is running there?—that one or this one?)
  2. Welche Tasche hast du? (Which bag do you have?—the red one? blue one?)
  3. Welches Kind spielt da? (Which child is playing there?—yours? mine?)

Normal Sentences

  1. Das ist welcher Hund? (That’s which dog?—like "Which one is this?")
  2. Sie hat welche Tasche! (She’s got which bag!—surprised.)

Easy Tips to Remember

  • Was für ein(e): Think "What’s it like?"—use ein for one thing, skip it for lots.
  • Welche(r): Think "Which one?"—no ein, just welcher/welche/welches.
  • Boy, Girl, Kid:
    • Boys (like Hund) = ein/welcher.
    • Girls (like Tasche) = eine/welche.
    • Kids (like Kind) = ein/welches.

  4. Was für Leute sind deine Freunde? (What kind of people are your friends?—fun?)
